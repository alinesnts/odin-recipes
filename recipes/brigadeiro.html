Brigadeiro

Brigadeiros are a beloved Brazilian sweet, traditionally made from condensed milk, cocoa powder, butter, and chocolate sprinkles. They have a rich, fudgy texture and a deep chocolate flavor, rolled into small bite-sized balls and often served in colorful paper cups. Simple in ingredients yet indulgent in taste, brigadeiros are a staple at birthday parties, celebrations, and family gatherings across Brazil.

More than just a dessert, brigadeiros carry a strong sense of nostalgia and comfort. They’re usually handmade, shared among friends, and enjoyed slowly—one sweet bite at a time. Whether classic or modernized with gourmet chocolate and creative toppings, brigadeiros remain a symbol of Brazilian warmth, celebration, and home-style sweetness.

Ingredients

  • 1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • chocolate sprinkles for rolling

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Combine condensed milk, cocoa, and butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Cook and stir until thickened, about 10 minutes.
  3. Transfer mixture into a shallow bowl and allow to cool.
  4. Pinch off some of the dough and roll into a small ball; place on a plate. Repeat with remaining mixture, then roll each brigadeiro in chocolate sprinkles.
  5. Enjoy or chill brigadeiros until ready to serve.
